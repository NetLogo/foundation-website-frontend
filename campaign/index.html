Help sustain the world's leading platform for understanding complex systems

What is Modeling Literacy and Why Does It Matter?

Models are how we understand the world. We use that understanding to solve problems and sometimes simply to enjoy understanding how things works. Often, our models are purely mental—representations of reality in our minds. But science relies on models that are more precise and reproducible, often using diagrams and mathematical equations.

In recent decades, computational models have become a driving force in science, engineering, and industry. They help us understand everything from how diseases spread through populations to how climate patterns shift, from how economies behave to how traffic flows through cities. These models reveal patterns and dynamics that would be invisible otherwise. The example below shows a simple NetLogo model of a forest fire. You can play around with it and hundreds of more models here.

Being modeling-literate means having the ability to create, understand, and critically evaluate models. Today's most pressing challenges emerge from large numbers of interacting parts, making them difficult to understand. We need better tools for making sense of complexity, and we need widespread literacy in using those tools.

Why NetLogo Matters

NetLogo is the world's most widely used agent-based modeling platform, serving both education and research. Created by Uri Wilensky in the late 1990s, NetLogo has a simple yet powerful design philosophy: "low threshold, high ceiling." Elementary school students can learn to use it, yet the same platform powers cutting-edge research.

The impact speaks for itself:

  • In education: it is by hundreds of thousands of students around the world and integrated into leading educational programs from OpenSciEd to Concord Consortium.
  • In research it has been used in over 10,000 peer-reviewed publications across diverse fields including epidemiology, biology, sociology, and economics.

But NetLogo's potential impact is still in early stages. Most people have never heard of NetLogo or agent-based modeling. For ABM to transform how society understands and addresses complex challenges, it needs to be widely accessible and sustainable.

How Your Gift Will Help

Your gift to the NetLogo Campaign is an investment in computational tools and modeling literacy for generations to come. Every dollar goes directly to the NetLogo Center through Northwestern University, supporting three critical priorities:

  1. Making NetLogo Even Better for Everyone, easier to use for beginners and even more powerful for advanced users.
  2. Building World-Class Learning Resources including science and social science curricula based around NetLogo modeling and professional development courses for educators.
  3. Creating an online platform for sharing and collaborating on NetLogo models and educational resources.

Every gift makes a difference. Whether you've learned with NetLogo, taught with it, or published with it—or you simply believe that modeling literacy is essential for our future—your support today ensures that students and researchers around the world can continue accessing free, powerful modeling tools for years to come.
